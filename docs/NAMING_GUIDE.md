# Naming Guide

To maintain a consistent world, all names for characters, locations, items, skills, and monsters must follow these standardized conventions.

## 1. Characters and NPCs
- **Naming Convention**: Elegant fantasy names. Avoid overly complex or unpronounceable names.
- **Titles**: Should be descriptive and capitalized (e.g., *Goddess of Peace*).
- **Nicknames**: Reserved for specific story elements.
- **Examples**:
  - Selenia (Moon Goddess)

## 2. Locations
Locations should evoke a sense of fantasy and mystery.
- **Villages/Towns**: Use *Town*, *Village*, or *City* (e.g., *Selestia Village*).
- **Dungeons/Wilderness**: Use *Forest*, *Ruins*, *Catacombs*, or *Cave* (e.g., *Whispering Forest*, *Darkroot Depths*).
- **Sacred Places**: Use *Sanctuary*, *Shrine*, or *Grove* (e.g., *Moon Sanctuary*, *Star Shrine*, *Ancient Grove*).

## 3. Items and Flora
Names should be descriptive and natural.
- **Flowers**: *Moon Hydrangea*, *Selenia's Moonflower* (NOT just "Moonflower" unless used colloquially).
- **Materials**: *Moon Essence*, *Treant Bark*.
- **Weapons**: Standardized medieval fantasy naming (e.g., *Iron Sword*, *Steel Longsword*).

## 4. Skills and Passives
Skills must use Title Case with spaces. Avoid camelCase or mashed words.
- **Do Use**: *Second Wind*, *Action Surge*, *Shield Wall*, *Blessing of Selenia*
- **Do NOT Use**: *ShieldWall*, *BlessingOfSelenia*, *passive_miner*

## 5. Monsters
Monsters follow a strict hierarchy and descriptive naming convention to easily identify their rank and type.
- **Hierarchy Examples**:
  - *Slime* (Base)
  - *Goblin Scout* (Low-tier)
  - *Goblin Archer* (Mid-tier ranged)
  - *Goblin Captain* (High-tier leader)
  - *Ancient Treant Sapling* (Boss)

## 6. UI Elements
Use consistent terminology across all menus.
- *Inventory*
- *Character Sheet*
- *Equipment*
- *Sanctuary*
- *Journal*
